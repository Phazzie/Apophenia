NEGATIVE EXAMPLES (What NOT to do):

Bad Example 1 - Too Generic:
"The protagonist experienced some scary events in a creepy location. Things got progressively worse. They made some choices. Now they're in danger and must decide what to do next."
Why this fails: No specific details, loses all atmosphere, could describe any horror story.

Bad Example 2 - Too Long:
"The story began when a memory researcher at a neuroscience facility started noticing strange things about their past. First, they found some photographs that didn't make sense. Then they talked to a colleague named Dr. Chen who said confusing things about their name. After that, more weird events happened involving memories and reflections. The facility seemed to have unusual properties affecting time and space. Eventually they realized..."
Why this fails: Rambling, exceeds word count, loses focus, dilutes horror.

Bad Example 3 - Loses Horror Tone:
"A researcher investigated memory problems at their lab. They found some inconsistencies in records and talked to coworkers about identity verification. Some unusual architectural features were noted. They're currently working to understand the situation better."
Why this fails: Clinical tone, no dread, sounds like a report not horror narrative.

Bad Example 4 - Missing Key Information:
"Strange things happened. Reality got weird. The character is confused now."
Why this fails: Too vague, loses all specific plot points, impossible to continue story from this.
